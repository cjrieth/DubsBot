       13579 A  3       T      500-720P   MLR  112      RYAN,JOEL                  Open      0/  40                      
                        COURSE DESCRIPTION:                                         
                        THIS CLASS WILL LOOK BACK AT THE                            
                        IMPACT OF THE COVID-19 PANDEMIC                             
                        ON CHILDREN AND FAMILIES, THE                               
                        PUBLIC POLICY DECISIONS OF LOCAL,                           
                        STATE, AND THE FEDERAL GOVERNMENT                           
                        LEADERS INCLUDING WHETHER TO KEEP                           
                        SCHOOLS CLOSED, SOCIAL SAFETY NET                           
                        PROGRAMS FOR LOW-INCOME KIDS AND                            
                        FAMILIES, COLLEGE GRANT DOLLARS,                            
                        HEALTH CARE, ACCESS TO CHILD CARE,                          
                        AND BASICALLY WHAT WORKED AND WHAT                          
                        DIDN'T. WE WILL THEN ZOOM AHEAD                             
                        TO 2024 AND EXAMINE THE CURRENT                             
                        IMPACTS ON CHILDREN AND FAMILIES                            
                        INCLUDING EDUCATIONAL OUTCOMES,                             
                        MENTAL HEALTH, CHILD WELFARE,                               
                        HOMELESSNESS, AND THE LATEST                                
                        FENTANYL CRISIS RAVAGING WA AND THE                         
                        US.
